Canon Summary

  • In-game date: 1832 AS Namal 6
  • Primary location: Road to Archaeological Dig Site
  • Review status: applied, applied 2026-05-05

Canon Events

  • At the rear of the caravan near the archaeological dig site, Sam touches Faban with a skeletal, decaying hand and deals necrotic damage. [Road to Archaeological Dig Site] (combat)
  • Faban puts on the protective green glass ring, feels its power flow over him, pushes Sam away, and draws his rapier. [Road to Archaeological Dig Site] (acquisition)
  • The noise draws the party into the fight; Corvinas, Bridgit, Richard, Gildas, Mikani, and Roon engage Sam and five hostile rangers at the rear of the caravan. [Road to Archaeological Dig Site] (combat)
  • Sam casts ancient evil magic that deals psychic damage to Corvinas and Faban, then cuts himself with a black dagger in an attempt that does not work as he intended. [Road to Archaeological Dig Site] (combat)
  • Corvinas falls during the battle after Sam reaches out with cursed hands, but Faban and Corvinas's own holy healing bring him back from the edge of death. [Road to Archaeological Dig Site] (combat)
  • Sam cuts through his own hand with the black dagger, spilling black blood along the blade and unleashing necrotic energy that summons two large foul-smelling frog-like creatures. [Road to Archaeological Dig Site] (combat)
  • Faban tells a magically empowered joke that makes Sam laugh uncontrollably, allowing Corvinas to strike the killing blow and sever Sam's arm. [Road to Archaeological Dig Site] (combat)
  • One summoned frog attacks Corvinas and knocks him down near death, while the other poisons Roon. [Road to Archaeological Dig Site] (combat)
  • Roon destroys one frog-like creature in a fury of blows, and Faban uses Cloud of Daggers to cut down the last frog-like creature. [Road to Archaeological Dig Site] (combat)
  • Faban searches Sam and finds the black dagger; Gildas determines it is necrotic in nature and probably from the Abyssal plane, while Corvinas notes a possible connection to the dig site's abyssal history. [Road to Archaeological Dig Site] (discovery)
  • The caravan reaches the archaeological dig site without further incident. [Archaeological Dig Site] (travel)
