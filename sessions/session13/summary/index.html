Session 13: Aftermath and parting of ways

Canon Summary

  • Physical date: 2025-10-12
  • In-game date: 1832 AS Namal 6, 1832 AS Namal 7, 1832 AS Namal 8, 1832 AS Namal 9, 1832 AS Namal 10
  • Primary location: Druid Retreat
  • Review status: applied, applied 2026-05-05

Canon Events

  • Gildas shows the obsidian hooked dagger to the archeologists; the blade is made for tearing flesh and has dried black blood on it, and Gildas stores it with the orb fragments and the Bentrios orb. [Archaeological Dig Site] (acquisition)
  • The party estimates it will take two to four days to reach the druid retreat, camps near the dig site, and Corvinas tells the party about being the last survivor of Mistrona after a demon attack. [Archaeological Dig Site] (social)
  • Corvinas describes the demon claw mark on his forehead; Roon recognizes the crossed-axes symbol as belonging to Polachnicindoya, lord of the seventh level of hell and demon of rage. [Archaeological Dig Site] (discovery)
  • The party sets out northward into the mountains toward the druid retreat and takes shelter from heavy rain in a shallow cave. [Mountain Road] (travel)
  • Faban plays Sally and the Good Day in the cave to lighten the mood during the storm. [Mountain Road] (social)
  • Cole appears at the cave with a magical deck of cards; Faban draws The Rogue, which Cole says harbingers betrayal, and Roon draws The Star, which signifies growth and evolution and makes him stronger. [Mountain Road] (discovery)
  • Mikani communes with nature in the mountains and senses the conspicuous absence of planar influence, no powerful beings influencing the area, traces of amethyst, iron, and obsidian in the earth, and residual evocation magic from spells cast long ago. [Mountain Road] (discovery)
  • As the party nears an encampment, vines trap Corvinas and an unseen archer fires a warning arrow with a parchment reading, "Those who seek, harm, or burn, shall face the wrath of many here." [Druid Retreat] (discovery)
  • Roon hacks at the vine to free Corvinas and is struck by an arrow that drains his strength. [Druid Retreat] (combat)
  • Faban calls out in greeting, and an ancient elvish archer named Jennifer appears, revealing herself as the object of the party's quest. [Druid Retreat] (social)
  • Jennifer says she no longer has the Wand of Wells because it was stolen recently amid broader magical failures and unreliable protection spells. [Druid Retreat] (discovery)
  • Jennifer identifies the known Wells: the Monastery of the Open Hand, Thataway, Henedal, the beaches to the east at Paramon, the sunken city of Catur, and the dwarven city of Balrog. [Druid Retreat] (discovery)
  • Jennifer explains that the Wells are kind, do not seek retribution, and are not inherently evil, and that stopping the Cataclysm requires seeking all of the Wells. [Druid Retreat] (discovery)
  • Jennifer leads the party into the Druid Retreat, also described as her coven, welcomes them to her patch of earth, says the situation is complicated, and notes that although she is over a thousand years old, many in the retreat can help the party. [Druid Retreat] (social)
  • Jennifer warns the party not to put full trust in the gods because the gods are flawed much like mortals. [Druid Retreat] (discovery)
