Roll the Barrel

Roll the Barrel, Boys
(A traditional hauling shanty)
[Shantyman – Verse 1]
The wind came hard from the northern foam,
The mast sang wild in the gale,
We’d been three weeks from a friendly shore
With nothing but salt and ale.
[Chorus – Crew]
Roll the barrel, boys, roll it round!
Heave her up from the harbor ground!
Drink it deep till the moon sinks low—
Roll the barrel, boys, yo ho ho!
[Shantyman – Verse 2]
The cook swore blind there was rum below
Locked tight in the captain’s hold,
But a thirsty crew with a thirst like ours
Won’t mind a lock of gold.
[Chorus – Crew]
Roll the barrel, boys, roll it round!
Heave her up from the harbor ground!
Drink it deep till the moon sinks low—
Roll the barrel, boys, yo ho ho!
[Shantyman – Verse 3]
The bosun growled when he heard the crash
And came with a rope in hand,
But a barrel loose on a rolling deck
Makes trouble hard to command.
[Chorus – Crew]
[Shantyman – Verse 4]
We rolled that cask through the midnight watch
While the captain slept below,
And by the time that the sun came up
There wasn’t a drop to show.
[Chorus – Crew]
[Shantyman – Verse 5]
Now if you sail with a thirsty crew
And hear that rolling sound,
You’d best grab hold of a mug, my friend—
Another barrel’s been found!
[Final Chorus – Crew]
Roll the barrel, boys, roll it round!
Heave her up from the harbor ground!
Drink it deep till the moon sinks low—
Roll the barrel, boys, yo ho ho!
